Approach to the 17th green at the Home Course, DuPont, WA

I have played golf since before I started grade school, and enjoy both the landscapes I walk through while playing and the unique challenges it provides. Each shot requires mentally making a plan and then being able to physically execute on it, and golf is at its most fun for me when a course requires a large variety of plans to make.

This map shows the golf courses I have played at.

Map key:

  • Defunct - Courses that are no longer operational; they are listed with the group they would be in otherwise.
  • Outside of memory - I have played these but do not remember much of them due to time passage.
  • Avoid - Courses I would decline to return to
  • Acceptable - Good for variety but nothing making me to want to go there specifically
  • Good - Come to mind as first options when deciding where to play
  • Great - Can be identified as memorable while playing
  • Outstanding - Courses to talk about as going somewhere to play them

Clicking the icon in the upper left-hand corner of the map below will provide a list broken down by category. Clicking on a course either on the map or on the list will bring up a description of its qualities (or lack thereof).
